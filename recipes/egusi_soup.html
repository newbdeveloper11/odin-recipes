Nigerian Egusi Soup


Description

Egusi soup is native to Nigerians in particular and West Africa in general.

Ground egusi seeds give the soup a unique flavor.

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup egusi seeds or pumpkin seeds
  • 1 ½ pounds cubed beef stew meat
  • salt to taste
  • ½ cup peanut oil
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 habanero peppers, seeded and minced
  • 2 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 18 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped

Steps to preparation

  1. Place seeds in a blender; blend until mixture is powdery, 30 to 40 seconds. Set aside.
  2. Cut beef into bite-sized cubes; season with salt.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  4. Cook beef in hot oil until brown but not cooked through, 3 to 5 minutes.
  5. Place tomatoes, onion, and peppers in a blender; blend until smooth, about 30 seconds.
  6. Stir tomato mixture into beef; reduce heat to medium-low and cover. Cook until meat is tender, 40 to 50 minutes.
  7. Add shrimp, tomato sauce, water, and tomato paste; simmer for 10 minutes.
  8. Stir in spinach and powdered seeds. Continue simmering for 10 more minutes.
